Executive Summary

Analysis of 9 major cities in Uttar Pradesh reveals a deeply concerning air quality crisis. Ghaziabad leads the pollution rankings with an AQI of 185, closely followed by Noida (179) and Dadri (155). Jhansi stands as the lone bright spot with the region's best AQI at 78 — a moderate-category outlier in a predominantly poor-to-unhealthy landscape. The average regional AQI of 148 is 2.5× the WHO safe threshold. Dadri's groundwater, drawing from the Western UP Indo-Gangetic plain, registers very hard water (TDS 600–950 ppm) with elevated fluoride, posing moderate-to-high risks for hair, scalp, and skin health. The combination of heavy particulate air pollution and hard water creates a compounded environmental health burden for residents of the NCR belt.

🔮 Key Anomaly & Surprise

Biggest Anomaly: Jhansi (AQI 78) is 2.4× cleaner than regional average (148). Despite being in the same state, its low industrial density and geographic position outside the NCR-Gangetic smog corridor creates a dramatically different air environment.
Most Surprising: Dadri's AQI peaks at 177 in early morning hours but drops to 90 mid-afternoon — a 97% intraday swing driven by boundary layer dynamics near the NTPC thermal power plant, suggesting hyper-local industrial cycles.
