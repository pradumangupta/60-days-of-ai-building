# Day 3 – Role-Based Prompting

## Objective

Explore how assigning different roles to Claude changes the quality and focus of responses.

## Question Used

How can I build an AI-powered study assistant for students with limited programming experience?

## Personas Tested

1. No Role
2. Founder
3. Developer

## Observations

### No Role Response

* General guidance
* Balanced overview
* Limited specialization

### Founder Response

* Focused on users
* Market validation
* Monetization opportunities
* Business strategy

### Developer Response

* Technical architecture
* Implementation details
* Tools and frameworks
* Development roadmap

## Key Learning

Role-Based Prompting significantly changes the perspective and depth of AI responses.

The Founder persona focused on business outcomes, while the Developer persona focused on execution and technical implementation.

## Claude Usage Counter

Installed and explored the Claude Usage Counter extension.

### Observations

* Displays message usage
* Helps track limits
* Useful for planning AI sessions

## Outcome

Learned how persona-based prompting can generate more targeted and useful responses depending on the goal.

Continue experimenting with advanced prompting techniques and practical AI workflows.
