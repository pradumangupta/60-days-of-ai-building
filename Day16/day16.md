# Day 16 – Build Your First Stock Research Skill

## Objective

Build a professional AI-powered Stock Fundamental Research Skill capable of analyzing Indian and global listed companies using business fundamentals, financial metrics, competitive positioning, valuation frameworks, and risk assessment.

The goal is to generate evidence-based research reports while avoiding investment advice, stock recommendations, or price targets.

---

# Project

## 📈 Stock Fundamental Research Skill

Today I designed a specialized AI research skill called:

**stock-fundamental-research**

The skill acts as a financial research assistant capable of analyzing listed companies using publicly available financial information and business fundamentals.

Unlike typical stock tips or prediction tools, this skill focuses exclusively on:

* Financial Statements
* Business Quality
* Competitive Advantages
* Valuation Analysis
* Risk Assessment
* Growth Drivers

---

# Problem Statement

Many investors focus only on stock prices while ignoring the underlying business fundamentals.

This project aims to create a structured framework that helps users understand:

* How a company earns money
* Business quality
* Financial strength
* Growth trends
* Key risks
* Industry position

without providing buy, sell, or hold recommendations.

# Interpretation Framework

## Debt Assessment

| D/E Ratio | Interpretation |
| --------- | -------------- |
| < 1       | Safe           |
| 1–2       | Moderate       |
| > 2       | Leveraged      |

---

## Return Ratios

| ROE / ROCE | Interpretation |
| ---------- | -------------- |
| >15%       | Good           |
| 10–15%     | Average        |
| <10%       | Weak           |

---

## Free Cash Flow

| Status             | Interpretation |
| ------------------ | -------------- |
| Positive & Growing | Strong         |
| Positive & Stable  | Stable         |
| Negative           | Concern        |

# Biggest Insight

The best stock analysis focuses on understanding businesses rather than predicting stock prices.

A strong company can still be a poor investment at the wrong valuation, and a weak company can appear attractive through superficial metrics.

Fundamental analysis requires balancing:

✅ Growth

✅ Profitability

✅ Financial Health

✅ Competitive Advantages

✅ Risks
